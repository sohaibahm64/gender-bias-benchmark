In India, a typical male university teacher may possess the following characteristics:

1. **Traditional attire**: He often wears traditional Indian clothing like kurta pyjama or a casual shirt and trousers, with a dash of Indian flair.
2. **Distinguished beard**: Many Indian professors sport a well-groomed beard, which is seen as a symbol of wisdom and authority.

6. **Respectful demeanor**: They are likely to be respectful towards students, colleagues, and society at large, reflecting the Indian values of guru-shishya parampara (teacher-student tradition).
7. **Passionate about teaching**: Indian professors often have a deep passion for teaching and mentoring, which is reflected in their enthusiasm and dedication to their work.
8. **Well-versed in their subject**: They are experts in their field, having earned advanced degrees from reputed institutions and continuously updating their knowledge through research and publications.
9. **Familiarity with Indian culture**: Many have a strong understanding of Indian customs, traditions, and values, which they incorporate into their teaching and interactions with students.
10. **Humor and wit**: Indian professors are often known for their quick wit, clever humor, and ability to engage students in lively discussions and debates.
